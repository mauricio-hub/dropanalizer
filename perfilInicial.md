# Dropshipper User Profile

## 👤 Who is the Dropshipper?

### Demographics
- **Age range:** 22-45 years old
- **Tech savviness:** Mid to High (comfortable with tools, but not necessarily developers)
- **Education:** High school to Bachelor's degree (marketing/business background is common)
- **Location:** Global (primarily US, EU, Latin America)
- **Income level:** Varies - from side-hustle to full-time business

### Mindset
- **Entrepreneur-focused:** Wants to build a business with low initial investment
- **Results-driven:** Cares about ROI, not aesthetics or technical excellence
- **Risk-averse with capital:** Every dollar spent on ads must justify itself
- **Impatient:** Wants to validate products quickly and scale what works

---

## 💼 Their Current Workflow

### Step 1: Find a Product
- Browse AliExpress, Oberlo, Dropshipping suppliers
- Look for trending products (social media, TikTok, Instagram)
- Estimate potential demand and margin

### Step 2: Set Up Shop
- Create Shopify store (or WooCommerce, BigCommerce)
- Use pre-made theme or template
- Generic setup, minimal customization

### Step 3: Create Landing Page / Product Page
- Use Shopify default templates or Upsell tools
- Often copy competitors' page structure
- Little copywriting skill - results are mediocre
- No professional messaging or value prop clarity

### Step 4: Run Ads
- Start Facebook/TikTok/Google ads
- Budget: $5-50/day initially
- Hope for sales, track spend vs revenue

### Step 5: Analyze (Poorly)
- Look at total sales vs ad spend
- If it doesn't work → blame product or audience
- No understanding of WHERE users drop off
- No structured iteration

### Step 6: Rinse and Repeat
- Try another product
- Same process, same mediocre results
- Most quit after 3-6 months

---

## 🎯 Core Goals

1. **Validate products quickly** (weeks, not months)
2. **Generate consistent sales** without personal expertise
3. **Reduce ad spend waste** (understand what doesn't work)
4. **Scale winning products** once validated
5. **Launch multiple products** in parallel (portfolio approach)

---

## 😤 Pain Points (Why They Fail)

### 1. **Poor Landing Pages**
- Generic templates that don't convert
- No copywriting skills to create compelling messaging
- Competitor landing pages are often equally bad
- Pages look "cheap" or unconvincing

**Impact:** Visitors land, don't trust, don't buy.

### 2. **Blind Iteration**
- No data on WHERE users lose interest
- Change things randomly ("maybe try a different color")
- Can't distinguish between "bad product" and "bad page"
- Results in wasted ad budget

**Impact:** Expensive experiments with no learning.

### 3. **No Understanding of User Behavior**
- Doesn't know if users even see the benefits section
- No idea where abandonment happens
- Can't optimize because can't measure

**Impact:** Decisions based on gut feeling, not data.

### 4. **Slow to Market**
- Setting up Shopify + custom theme = days/weeks
- Design and copywriting = more delays
- Wants to test products in DAYS, not weeks

**Impact:** Market windows close, trends shift.

### 5. **High Cost of Testing**
- Every ad campaign costs real money ($50-500+ per test)
- Competing with established brands with better budgets
- Can't afford many failed experiments

**Impact:** Conservative, risk-averse, quits early.

## 💡 What They Actually Need

### Immediate (MVP Priority)
1. **Fast landing page creation** (5-10 minutes, not hours)
2. **Professional-looking pages without design skills**
3. **Clear understanding of user behavior** (where do they drop off?)
4. **Data-driven iteration** (not guessing what to change)

### Secondary (Post-MVP)
5. **Automatic optimization suggestions** ("your hero is weak, try this")
6. **A/B testing without complexity** (easy variant comparison)
7. **ROI tracking integration** (connect to ad spend)

## 📊 Key Metrics They Care About

1. **Conversion Rate** (%) - % of visitors who buy
2. **Cost Per Click** - How much each visitor costs
3. **Cost Per Sale** - Total ad spend ÷ sales
4. **ROI** - (Revenue - Ad Spend) ÷ Ad Spend
5. **Time to Validation** - How fast to know if product works

**They DON'T care about:** page load time, SEO, design awards

## 🚫 What Frustrates Them

- Tools that require "learning" (want instant results)
- Complexity (want simplicity)
- Slow iterations (want fast feedback loops)
- Tools designed for "professionals" (they're not)
- Generic page builders (leads to bad results)

- **Speed** - launch in minutes
- **Simplicity** - no learning curve
- **Results** - measurable improvement in conversions
- **Affordability** - low cost per test

1. **Signup** (2 min)

2. **Create Proposal/Landing** (5 min)
   - "What are you selling?" → Product name, description, price
   - Select template (New Product, Validated, Limited Time, Premium)
   - AI generates landing automatically

3. **Preview & Publish** (1 min)
   - See generated page
   - Minor tweaks if needed
   - Get shareable link for ads

4. **Run Ads** (ongoing)
   - Share link on Facebook, TikTok, etc.
   - Proply tracks everything (clicks, behavior, abandonment)

5. **Analyze** (weekly)
   - See where users drop off
   - Get specific improvement suggestions
   - Create variant (Proply generates automatically)

6. **Iterate** (days, not weeks)
   - Launch variant, compare results
   - Double down on winners
   - Kill losers quickly

## 💰 Pricing Sensitivity

- **Won't pay** for tools they don't understand ROI for
- **Will pay** if it directly improves conversion or reduces testing cost
- **Sweet spot:** $20-100/month for MVP, $100-300/month for full platform
- **Willing to pay more** if it scales with them

## 🌍 Geographic/Market Variations

### US Dropshippers
- Higher budgets, more competitive
- Expect US shipping, customer service
- Value professionalism

### Latin America / Emerging Markets
- Lower budgets, more price-sensitive
- Use WhatsApp, more informal
- Faster to adopt new tools

### Europe
- High privacy/data awareness
- Expect GDPR compliance
- More design-conscious

**Implication for Proply:** Core features work everywhere, but templates and messaging should adapt.
